## Reasoning + Response
We are asked to compare the intensities of two earthquakes from their Richter magnitudes.

On the Richter scale,
\[
M=\log_{10}\left(\frac{I}{I_0}\right)
\]
So the difference in magnitudes gives
\[
M_1-M_2=\log_{10}\left(\frac{I_1}{I_2}\right).
\]

For the two earthquakes:
\[
8.3-5.1=3.2
\]
Thus
\[
\log_{10}\left(\frac{I_1}{I_2}\right)=3.2
\]
and
\[
\frac{I_1}{I_2}=10^{3.2}.
\]

Now,
\[
10^{3.2}=10^3\cdot 10^{0.2}=1000\cdot 10^{0.2}\approx 1000(1.585)=1585.
\]

So the San Francisco earthquake was about 1585 times as intense.
