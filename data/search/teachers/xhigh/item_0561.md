We need the prime factorization of \(30!\).

Use Legendre’s formula:

\[
v_p(30!)=\left\lfloor \frac{30}{p}\right\rfloor+\left\lfloor \frac{30}{p^2}\right\rfloor+\cdots
\]

\[
v_2=15+7+3+1=26
\]
\[
v_3=10+3+1=14
\]
\[
v_5=6+1=7
\]
\[
v_7=4
\]

The remaining primes from \(11\) to \(30\) contribute:

\[
11^2,\ 13^2,\ 17,\ 19,\ 23,\ 29
\]

So

\[
30! = 2^{26}\cdot 3^{14}\cdot 5^7\cdot 7^4\cdot 11^2\cdot 13^2\cdot 17\cdot 19\cdot 23\cdot 29
\]

None of the options is exactly correct, but option E appears to be the intended closest match.
